Patience Agyiri Andoh

Inspired by the loss of my mother to cancer, I am committed to saving lives through science, research, and education. Through the Cancer Biology Series and my academic journey, I hope to inform, inspire, and improve lives in Ghana and beyond.

Education

I am currently pursuing a BSc. in Biological Sciences at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), Kumasi – Ghana. Ranked in the top 15% of my class, I have earned academic honors in Biochemistry, Immunology, and Microbiology.

Research

My research work explores pressing biological challenges in Ghana and globally. I have led a capstone project on mercury exposure among gold miners and investigated microbial diversity in the environment. I am also passionate about cancer biology and actively develop public education tools through the Cancer Biology Series initiative.

Work

My hands-on experience includes internships at Holy Trinity Medical Center and Kinapharma Ltd., where I contributed to research, blood analysis, and pharmaceutical quality control. These roles sharpened my lab skills, from CBC testing to chromatographic analysis.

Community Service

As General Secretary of the KNUST Debate Society and former Deputy Head of the Library Committee, I’ve led initiatives to empower students academically and socially. I also mentor junior biological science students and develop outreach projects, such as my Cancer Biology Series, to promote awareness and health literacy.
